What is K-pop?

K-pop, short for Korean pop music, is a genre of popular music originating from South Korea. It's characterized by catchy melodies, high-production music videos, and intricate choreography, often performed by "idol" groups consisting of trained singers and dancers.
K-pop draws influence from various genres like pop, hip-hop, R&B, and electronic music, and has become a global cultural phenomenon with a dedicated international fanbase.
The roots of K-pop began in the 1950s, however, and have since then already been influenced a lot by different types of Western music and pop groups. American pop music culture has especially had - and still does have - an effect on K-Pop.
