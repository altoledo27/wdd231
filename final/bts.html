BTS

BTS (Bangtan Boys) is a globally renowned South Korean boy band known for blending energetic dance-pop and hip-hop with introspective lyrics on topics like mental health and self-empowerment. Formed by producer Bang Si Hyuk and debuting in 2013 with their "School Trilogy," the septetâ€”RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkookâ€”quickly gained international recognition and built a massive fan base known as ARMY.

Their breakthrough came with the Love Yourself series, leading to multiple chart-topping albums worldwide, including Love Yourself: Tear, Answer, and Map of the Soul: 7. In 2020, they made history as the first all-South Korean act to top the U.S. singles chart with "Dynamite." BTS continued dominating global charts with hits like "Butter" and "My Universe" and solidified their cultural impact through awards, philanthropic appearances (including a visit to the White House), and contributions to Korea's economy.

Known for their self-written lyrics and creative involvement, BTS has released a mix of Korean, Japanese, and English-language music. In 2022, they marked their 10th anniversary with the anthology album Proof. Their influence extends beyond music, setting records and redefining what it means to be a global pop phenomenon.
